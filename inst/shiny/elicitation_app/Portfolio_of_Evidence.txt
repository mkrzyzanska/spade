Identification:
As/Dupondius of Sabina, RIC Hadrian 2496, struck in Rome 128-129 (https://numismatics.org/ocre/id/ric.2_3(2).hdn.2496)

Wear = 2, although a definite determination is difficult due to the corroded state of the coin.

It is uncertain if the coin is an as or a dupondius, but both denominations belong to the Augustan coinage system, which finally collapsed around 260 CE. Coins of these denominations almost completely disappeared from circulation then.

Such coins circulated widely in Britannia.
